UO Advancement Donor Engagement Analysis

A summary of an Advancement Leadership Internship project focused on outreach engagement, aggregate response patterns, and metric design.

Project Overview

During my University of Oregon Advancement Leadership Internship, I worked with donor engagement and recognition data to better understand how recipients responded to a stewardship outreach effort. The project emphasized aggregate response behavior, metric development, and practical recommendations for more targeted communication.

This public version keeps the project summary at the aggregate level while I confirm what details are appropriate to publish. The focus here is the analytical work: turning outreach behavior into a clearer framework for evaluating engagement and tailoring future communication.

Index

Built a scoring approach that captured more than a simple completed-or-not-completed outcome.

Signals

Compared response behaviors such as opening, clicking, repeat engagement, and other high-intent actions.

Strategy

Connected aggregate engagement patterns to recommendations for more targeted outreach.

Engagement Index

The core idea was to treat engagement as a spectrum. The index scored delivery issues, message opens, activity completion stages, and additional high-intent behaviors so that the project could identify meaningful response patterns beyond a simple completed-or-not-completed outcome.

< 0 Delivery issue
0-1 Did not engage
1 Opened message
1-2 Engaged partially
2 Completed activity
> 2 Substantive engagement
-1 Delivery problem

Why It Mattered

1

Completion Was Not the Whole Story

The project looked beyond a single completion rate to identify other behaviors that could signal interest.

2

Behavior Added Context

Aggregate response patterns helped show where message design, timing, or format could shape participation.

3

Segmentation Became More Useful

Engagement signals offered a way to think about audience groups by responsiveness, not only by broad category.

4

Recommendations Became Actionable

The final work connected analysis to practical next steps for clearer, more targeted stewardship communication.
